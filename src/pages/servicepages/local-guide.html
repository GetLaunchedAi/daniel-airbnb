Explore the Magic of Orlando


More Than Just Theme Parks


While Orlando is famous for its theme parks, the "City Beautiful" offers so much more. From pristine golf courses and world-class shopping to beautiful nature preserves and vibrant arts districts, there's something for everyone. Whether you're here for a family vacation, a romantic getaway, or a business trip, our local guide helps you make the most of your stay.



Top Attractions

  1. Walt Disney World Resort: The Magic Kingdom, Epcot, Hollywood Studios, and Animal Kingdom.
  2. Universal Orlando Resort: Universal Studios, Islands of Adventure, and Volcano Bay.
  3. International Drive: The Icon Park wheel, Madame Tussauds, and Sea Life Aquarium.

Hidden Gems & Local Favorites

  • Lake Eola Park: Swan boats and a farmers market in the heart of downtown.
  • Wekiwa Springs State Park: Kayaking, swimming, and hiking in natural Florida springs.
  • Winter Park: Scenic boat tours and upscale shopping on Park Avenue.
  • East End Market: A neighborhood market with local food and goods.

Orlando Travel FAQs

Common questions to help you plan your perfect Orlando vacation.

  • Orlando is a year-round destination, but the best weather is typically from October to May. Summers are warm and perfect for water parks, while winters are mild and pleasant.

  • While rideshare apps are widely available, a rental car offers the most flexibility for visiting multiple parks, dining areas, and beaches on either coast.

  • You can reach the Atlantic coast (Cocoa Beach, Daytona Beach) in about an hour, and the Gulf Coast (Clearwater, St. Pete Beach) in about 90 minutes.
